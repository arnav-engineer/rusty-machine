1. Abstract (200 to 350 words)
Your entire paper in brief. Highlight problem, method, key results, and impact.
Core Problem → What issue is being addressed & why it
Approach → How the problem was studied
Key Findings → Main results with clear meaning
Impact → Bold statement on implications

 2. Introduction (800 to 1200 words)
Set the context, define the gap, and clearly state your contribution.
Hook → Start with a fact, problem, or question
Current Knowledge → Summarize field advances
Gap → What’s missing/unknown & why critical
Objective & Hypothesis → State research question

 3. Literature Review / Integration (1200 to 1800 words)
Show what exists, what is missing, and how your work advances the field.
Framework → Theories/models guiding your study
Contrasts → How it aligns, extends, or challenges existing
Evidence Gaps → Highlight what’s overlooked
Importance → Why filling this gap matters

 4. Methods (1000 to 1500 words)
Explain your approach with clarity and reproducibility.
Design → Why this approach fits your question
Population/Sample → Inclusion/exclusion criteria
Tools & Protocols → Instruments, measures, techniques
Analysis Plan → Data analysis strategy, tests, software

 5. Results (800 to 1500 words)
Present findings logically with strong visuals and no unnecessary interpretation.
Flow → Start descriptive, move to critical analysis
Visuals → Why they matter for the field
Limitations → Weaknesses of your study
Conclusion → Wrap up main points, pose questions

 6. Discussion (800 to 1200 words)
Interpret results, connect to literature, and highlight implications.

 7. Conclusion (300 to 600 words)
Summarize contributions and outline future directions.
